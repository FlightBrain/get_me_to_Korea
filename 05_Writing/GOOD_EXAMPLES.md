## What Qualifies as "Good"

### Cold Email:
- ✓ Reply rate >15% (vs. 5-10% baseline)
- ✓ Multiple positive responses
- ✓ At least one qualified meeting booked
- ✓ Specific feedback that you can learn from

### LinkedIn Message:
- ✓ Response rate >25% (vs. 15-20% baseline)
- ✓ Genuine conversation (not 1-word reply)
- ✓ Person engaged with your profile or stayed in message thread

### Post:
- ✓ Engagement rate >5% on LinkedIn (vs. 3-4%)
- ✓ Engagement rate >2% on X (vs. 1-1.5%)
- ✓ Meaningful comments, not just emoji reactions
- ✓ Visible lift in profile views

### Research Summary:
- ✓ Stakeholder acted on it (sent to prospect, shaped strategy, answered a question)
- ✓ Multiple people requested or shared it
- ✓ Added a novel insight (not just compiled facts)

## Meta Patterns (Across All Content)

### What Actually Works:
- **Specificity beats generality.** "I noticed you're building agents" > "You're doing cool AI stuff"
- **Vulnerability beats polish.** "We got this wrong..." > "Best practices are..."
- **Clear ask beats vague ask.** "15 minutes next Tuesday?" > "Let's connect sometime"
- **Single insight beats multiple.** One strong point > three mediocre ones
- **Your voice beats corporate voice.** "I think..." > "Leveraging our solution..."

### What Doesn't Work:
- Generic openers that could apply to anyone
- Fake urgency ("limited spots," "ending soon")
- Asking for something before building rapport
- Compliments that sound insincere ("Your product is amazing!")
- Anything that looks like a template
