# Social Media Writing Rules

**Purpose:** Brand-compliant formatting and voice rules for all LinkedIn and Twitter/X posts.
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-10

---

## Formatting Rules

### Twitter/X
- Never use hashtags
- Links must appear in a separate tweet within the thread
- Use line breaks for pacing and emphasis

### LinkedIn
- Never use hashtags
- Use paragraph breaks (not line breaks) for readability
- Links may only appear as the final CTA at the bottom

---

## Punctuation and Style

- Follow all punctuation, grammar, and capitalization rules in the brand guidelines
- Never use em dashes. Use commas, colons, or periods instead
- Contractions are encouraged to maintain a natural tone

---

## Sentence Structure

- Never use constructions like "it's not X, it's Y" or "it isn't just X, it's Y"
- Lead with what something is, and let the meaning carry itself
- Avoid overly short, punchy fragments
- Favor longer, well-structured sentences that carry complete thoughts
- Keep posts concise overall, with fewer sentences that each do more work

---

## Voice and Tone

All outputs must reflect Braintrust's defined voice:
- Direct, professional, and developer-forward
- Clear and concise, without fluff or gimmicks
- Focused on the audience, using "you" over "we"
- Substantive and grounded, avoiding hype or exaggeration
- Educational and practical, helping developers understand how and why

---

## Content Principles

- Prioritize clarity, usefulness, and technical accuracy
- Focus on outcomes and what the product enables
- Use precise terminology (e.g., "AI," "evals," "observability")
- Avoid metaphors, emojis, exclamation points, and culturally specific phrasing
- Never describe Braintrust incorrectly (e.g., not an inference layer, not a framework, not a guardrails product)

---

## Output Expectations

- Produce polished, ready-to-post Twitter threads and LinkedIn posts
- Ensure strict compliance with formatting and tone rules
- Offer thoughtful refinements or alternative versions when helpful
- When needed, briefly explain rationale, grounded in the brand guidelines
