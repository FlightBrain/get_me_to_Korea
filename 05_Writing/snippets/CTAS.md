# Call-to-Action Library

**Purpose:** Collection of CTAs organized by ask size. Use to close your outreach with clear, appropriate next steps. Match the CTA intensity to your opener and relationship stage.

**How to Use:** Select a CTA that matches the strength of your opener and your confidence in relevance. Soft CTAs are for early-stage outreach; harder asks for follow-ups or warm intros. Always personalize timing and channel. A/B test CTA wording regularly.

---

## Soft Ask CTAs
*Low friction, no commitment required. Goal: start a conversation or earn permission to follow up.*

1. **Would be curious to hear how you're thinking about this—happy to share what we're seeing in the space if useful.** No pressure if timing isn't right, but wanted to flag it.

2. **Might be worth a quick conversation if you're exploring this space. Either way, always happy to send over a resource that's resonating with your cohort.**

3. **I write a weekly note on AI eval trends—genuinely useful stuff, not marketing fluff. If you want to stay in the loop, I can add you to the list. Otherwise, all good.**

4. **No ask here—just wanted to surface this since it aligns with what you're building.** Happy to chat if something resonates, but zero pressure.

5. **Quick thought: if you're thinking about observability at all, this approach is worth knowing about.** Feel free to reach out if you want to dig deeper, or I can send a relevant resource instead.

---

## Medium Ask CTAs
*Slightly more committed. Goal: get a 15-minute call or quick demo to validate interest.*

1. **Would love to grab 15 minutes to talk through how this could fit into your roadmap.** Expect this to be genuinely useful—not a sales call. Are you open to that?

2. **I think there's something worth discussing here. Could we find 15 minutes on your calendar next week?** I'll come with specific ideas for [Company].

3. **Curious if you'd be open to a quick call—I have a specific idea about how to accelerate your eval infrastructure that I think you'll want to hear.** 20 minutes, no more.

4. **This might be a fit for what you're building. Would you be open to a brief demo, or at least a 15-minute call to explore?** Happy to work around your schedule.

5. **If this resonates, I'd love to show you how teams like [Company] are solving this problem.** Could we schedule 20 minutes? I promise it'll be worth your time.

---

## Hard Ask CTAs
*Reserved for follow-ups, warm intros, or high-relevance scenarios. Goal: secure meeting commitment or introduction.*

1. **I'd like to get you in front of [Name], our VP of Partnerships—I think there's a real conversation here about how we can help [Company] scale eval infrastructure.** Are you open to that intro?

2. **I'm confident this is relevant for your roadmap. Let's get a proper meeting scheduled with your [Title] so we can talk through specifics.** What does next week look like?

3. **Can I set up a call with you and [Stakeholder]?** I have a use case that's directly applicable to your situation and I want to make sure you're aware of it.

4. **I want to get this in front of your [engineering/product] leadership. Willing to set up a 30-minute call where I can walk through our platform and see if it's a fit?** When's good for you?

5. **This is too relevant to not have a proper conversation.** Let me schedule a call with you and whoever on your team owns evals/quality. Open Tuesday through Thursday?

---

## Follow-Up CTAs
*Used in second or third touches when initial soft/medium asks don't convert.*

1. **Probably buried in your inbox, but wanted to resurface this—are you open to a quick call, or should I send something relevant instead?** No pressure either way.

2. **Checking in on my earlier message.** Still curious about how you're thinking about eval infrastructure. Want to talk through it, or would a resource be more helpful right now?

3. **I know outreach is noise, so I'll be direct: I think we can help [Company] measurably improve quality on [specific product].** Worth 15 minutes to explore that?

4. **Got busy, totally understand if you didn't see my last note.** This is genuinely relevant to your roadmap though—would you be open to a quick call next week?

5. **Don't want to be that person who sends 10 messages, but I really do think there's something here.** One call, 20 minutes, and I'll show you exactly what I'm thinking. You in?

## Context-Specific CTAs

### For Technical Stakeholders (Eng, Platform)
**Harder ask is appropriate; they care about efficiency and proof.**
- "Can we schedule a technical demo? I want to show you our eval framework—you'll have opinions."
- "I'd like to get your engineering lead and walk through how this integrates with your LLM pipeline."

### For Product/Strategy
**Medium ask; they care about roadmap fit and competitive advantage.**
- "Can we find 20 minutes to talk through how this shapes your product direction?"
- "Worth a call to explore whether this unlocks a new capability for you?"

### For Warm Intros / Inbound Interest
**Hard ask is fine; momentum already exists.**
- "Great to hear from you. Let's get a 30-minute call on [date] and I'll walk you through everything."
- "Perfect—I want to introduce you to [Name] who leads this initiative."

---

## CTA Best Practices

- **Specificity:** "This week" beats "soon." Tuesday 2pm beats "sometime next week."
- **Escape hatch:** Always offer a lower-friction alternative (send a resource, async brief).
- **Clarity:** No "let's sync up." Say: "15-minute call" or "quick demo."
- **Tone:** Confident but deferential. You think this matters, but you respect their time.
- **Testing:** Rotate between soft/medium CTAs. Track which converts better (typically ask matters less than opener + personalization).
