# Email & Message Openers (Real Braintrust Messaging)

**Purpose:** Proven opening lines for cold outreach to Braintrust ICP. Each opener anchors to real signals from ICP doc (engineers, VPs, PMs) and uses actual customer language.

**How to Use:** Choose an opener matching your research and persona. Customize company/person name and specific signal. Never send as-is. Test in batches of 10+ to measure response rates. Track what works per persona.

---

## Signal-Based Openers (FOR ENGINEERS)
*Triggered by hiring, job postings, technical blog posts, GitHub activity.*

1. **Saw you hired for "AI Quality Engineer" last month.** That role usually gets buried rebuilding eval infrastructure. How are you planning to do that differently?

2. **Your engineering blog post on "scaling LLM testing" got my attention.** Multi-model evaluation is the hard part. What's your current approach for testing OpenAI + Anthropic in parallel?

3. **Noticed [Person] joined as Staff AI Engineer at [Company].** That's usually the point where eval infrastructure becomes critical. Quick question: are you planning to build or buy that infrastructure?

4. **Your GitHub repo shows you're already thinking about eval frameworks.** Question: how much engineering time are you spending maintaining that internally?

5. **Saw your Series B raised $40M with "scaling AI infrastructure" mentioned.** Early-stage eval framework decisions usually compound. Worth a quick conversation?

---

## Signal-Based Openers (FOR PMs & EXECUTIVES)
*Triggered by funding, product launches, executive moves, press coverage.*

1. **Your Series B just closed. Congrats. Question: as you're scaling AI features, how are you planning to keep quality from becoming a bottleneck?**

2. **Saw your product launch announcement mentioning the new AI feature.** How are you evaluating quality now that it's live? (asking because most teams hit a wall here)

3. **Noticed you hired a new VP of AI/ML last quarter.** That's usually when governance and eval become CEO-level conversations. Relevant?

4. **Your earnings announcement mentioned "AI driving 30% of revenue growth." How are you evaluating that quality systematically? Asking because Zapier hit exactly this question at similar growth.**

5. **Fortune 500 just announced "AI Center of Excellence."** Evaluating models across 50+ business units is hard without centralized infrastructure. Worth exploring?

---

## Insight-Based Openers (PATTERN RECOGNITION)
*Share a real pattern from your domain. Position as knowledgeable observer, not salesperson.*

1. **I've been noticing a pattern across 40+ AI-first companies: the ones scaling fastest aren't better at prompting. They're better at measuring what matters. Could that be relevant for [Company]?**

2. **Most teams shipping AI right now hit the same wall: can ship features fast, but quality assurance becomes bottleneck within 6 months. Curious if that's on your radar?**

3. **Companies that started with eval infrastructure early (Series A, not Series C) are shipping at 3x velocity by the time they hit scale. Worth a 15-min conversation?**

4. **Quick observation: 70 engineers at Notion can deploy models in <24 hours. How? Eval infrastructure is part of the CI/CD pipeline, not an afterthought. Is that your model?**

5. **The ones winning in production AI right now aren't the best at model selection. They're the best at catching regressions before users find them. How are you approaching that?**

---

## Question-Based Openers (CURIOSITY HOOKS)
*Lead with genuine curiosity. Show lightweight research.*

1. **Quick question: as you scale your AI team, how are you planning to test model updates without killing your shipping velocity?**

2. **I'm curious about your current workflow: when you ship a new LLM feature, how long does it take to validate quality before production?**

3. **How are you currently handling multi-model testing?** (OpenAI, Anthropic, Cohere, etc.—asking because approaches vary wildly)

4. **What's your evaluation process look like right now?** Spreadsheets, custom tooling, or something else?

5. **When you're evaluating whether Claude 3.5 is better than GPT-4, how do you actually measure that?** (I'm asking because most teams say "we just hope.")

---

## Event-Based Openers (HumanX, Dinners, Industry Conferences)
*Primary hook is the event itself.*

1. **I noticed you're interested in HumanX in May. We have an extra VIP pass—would be great to connect beforehand. What's the biggest AI evaluation challenge you're tackling right now?**

2. **Saw you registered for the AI Infrastructure Summit. We'll be there too. Want to grab coffee before the keynotes? I'm curious about your eval strategy.**

3. **You're at the Warriors game tonight (Braintrust dinner). Let's connect and chat about what you're building with AI.**

---

## Direct Openers (FOR KNOWN PROBLEMS)
*Acknowledge their specific situation, show understanding, ask one thing.*

1. **Your team is shipping retrieval-augmented AI and I have one question: how are you evaluating accuracy end-to-end before production? We've solved that for teams like Carta and Dropbox.**

2. **You're evaluating models for your core product. Decision: how do you know if new model is actually better, or just different? We help teams answer that systematically.**

3. **You're scaling your AI team. Usual constraint: eval infrastructure becomes bottleneck within 6 months. Worth a quick conversation about how others are solving it?**

4. **I work with teams at companies like Notion and Zapier. One universal pattern: everyone underinvests in evals until users complain. You already thinking about this?**

5. **You're building AI-native products. The question that always comes up: "How do we catch regressions before customers do?" We have a framework for that.**

---

## Notes

- **Best performers:** Signal-based (for engineers) + Question-based (all personas) + Event-driven (high response rate)
- **Response rate targets:** Signal-based 10-12%, Question-based 8-10%, Event-driven 20-25%
- **Personalization baseline:** Always include 1-2 specific signals (hiring, product launch, blog post, GitHub activity)
- **Length:** 2-4 sentences max. Goal is curiosity, not pitch.
- **Tone:** Professional but conversational. Match their language, not corporate-speak.
