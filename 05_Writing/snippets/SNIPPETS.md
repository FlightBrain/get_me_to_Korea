## Pain Descriptions

### The Manual Testing Problem
Right now, you're probably doing QA the old way: spinning up a spreadsheet, getting stakeholders to manually test edge cases, hoping you caught the bad ones. It works until you don't.

### The Scaling Problem
At 5 features, manual testing is fine. At 50 features? At 500 features? You're either hiring a QA team that grows linearly with your product, or you're shipping bugs to production.

### The Measurement Gap
You can see that users like your feature, but you don't know why. Was it the LLM output quality? The UX? The relevance? This uncertainty makes iteration slow and risky.

### The Production Surprise
Features work in your staging environment. Then they hit production with real data, real scale, real complexity. And they break in ways your manual testing never caught.

### The Velocity Tax
Every new LLM feature needs testing. Right now, that testing takes two weeks. With proper observability infrastructure, it would take two days. That's what you're paying for by not having this.

## Weak Phrases to Avoid

- "Best-in-class" (meaningless)
- "Cutting-edge" (proves nothing)
- "Synergy" (pretentious)
- "Increase efficiency" (vague)
- "Leverage our platform" (corporate)
