# Post Ideas & Hooks

**Purpose:** Repository of content ideas for LinkedIn and X (Twitter). Stores the hook, angle, and execution details so you can quickly turn ideas into posts without losing momentum.

**How to Use:** Pick an idea that aligns with recent industry events or your current focus. Customize the hook with recent examples. Post one idea per week on LinkedIn; 2-3 per week on X. Track which perform best (engagement rate, profile views, DM opens). Move high performers to GOOD_EXAMPLES.md.

## Post Idea 1: The LLM Reliability Paradox

**Idea:** Teams are shipping AI features at record speed but with terrible quality controls.

**Angle:** Speed and reliability are not trade-offs if you measure properly.

**Hook:** "We shipped 47 LLM-powered features last year. Guess how many we caught quality issues on before customers did? Three."

**Target Audience:** Engineering leaders, product managers, AI practitioners at Series A-C companies

**Format:** LinkedIn (long-form), X (hook + thread)

**Status:** Ready to post

**Supporting Points:**
- Manual testing doesn't scale. You can't manually QA 50 features.
- Observability in production is non-negotiable. You ship bugs if you skip it.
- First mover advantage: most teams don't have this figured out yet.

**Hook:** "Your LLM works great with your test data. Real users have different data, different intentions, different edge cases. That's where it breaks."

**Target Audience:** Product managers, engineering teams, anyone shipping AI features to users

**Format:** LinkedIn (tactical insights), X (observation + follow-up)

**Status:** Ready to post

**Supporting Points:**

- The bugs that cost money are the ones you don't see until customers report them.

## Engagement Tactics

### For LinkedIn:
- Lead with vulnerability ("We got this wrong...")
- Offer specific numbers
- Include a question at the end to drive comments
- Post Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am ET

### For X:
- Hook first, argument second
- Use threads to expand ideas
- Engage with others' hot takes
- Post 1-2x daily when actively building momentum

### General:
- Always cite examples (but anonymize if needed)
- Make insights actionable
- Avoid jargon; explain terms
- Respond to early comments within first hour
