# LinkedIn Index

## Purpose
Centralized approach to LinkedIn outreach: connection requests, follow-up messaging, engagement strategy, and DM templates. LinkedIn is best used for warm-up before other channels, not as primary cold outreach.

## How to Use
1. Research prospect on LinkedIn (check their recent posts, activity, job title)
2. Use Connection Template to craft personalized connection request
3. After acceptance, move to engagement (comments on their posts)
4. After 3–7 days of engagement, move to DM with specific value offer
5. Log all activity in the tracking section below

---

## Connection Request Strategy

**Timing:** Send connection request with message, not blank.

**What to include in request message:**
- Your name and reason for connecting (specific)
- One compliment or relevant observation
- Nothing asking for anything yet

**Targeting before connection:**
- Recent activity (posted in last 2 weeks)
- Title match (VP Eng, Head of AI, CTO, etc.)
- Company matches ICP
- Evidence they're active on platform (comments, posts, engagement)

## Connection Request Templates

### **Template A: Technical Respect**
*Use for CTOs, VPs of Eng, Head of AI—people who post technical content*

```
Hi [Name],

Saw your talk at [Conference] on [topic]—the part about [specific insight] was really solid.

Would be good to connect and follow your thinking on where evaluations go next.

---
Kensington
```

**Why it works:** Shows you listened to specific content. "Follow your thinking" is a genuine compliment, not a sales hook. No ask.

---

### **Template B: Hiring Signal**
*Use when they just posted a job or got hired into a new role*

```
Hi [Name],

Congrats on the [new role/hiring for AI]. Building teams at [company's stage] is interesting—usually the evaluation infrastructure question gets thornier as you scale. Worth connecting.

---
Kensington
```

**Why it works:** Acknowledges the career moment. You're not congratulating a promotion for promotion's sake—you're showing you understand the problem it surfaces.

---

### **Template C: Product Announcement**
*Use when they shipped something new or announced a launch*

```
Hi [Name],

Saw the announcement about [product feature]. Multi-model orchestration is going to be table stakes soon. Curious to follow how [Company] approaches the measurement side.

---
Kensington
```

**Why it works:** You're not pitching—you're saying "I'm interested in how you solve this specific problem." That's genuine interest.

## Post-Connection Engagement (Days 1–7)

**Strategy:** Build relationship through comments and small engagement before asking for anything.

### **Engagement Approach**

1. **Find their recent posts** (LinkedIn feed, posts on profile)
2. **Leave thoughtful comments** (1–2 per week)
   - NOT: "Great post!" or generic praise
   - YES: Specific insight that extends their thought, question that shows you're thinking about the problem, or personal experience that validates their point
3. **Reference their post in comment** (shows you actually read it, not bot engagement)
4. **No mention of Braintrust** (this phase is pure relationship)

### **Comment Examples**

**Their post:** "Just shipped agent orchestration layer. Now the hard part—testing."

**Your comment:**
```
This. The orchestration logic is where most teams find out their eval approach was incomplete. One layer of abstraction and suddenly you need to test a totally different thing.

How are you thinking about the measurement side? Are you testing the orchestrator separately or end-to-end?
```

**Why it works:** You're showing expertise. You're asking a real question. You're demonstrating that you understand the depth of the problem.

---

**Their post:** "Hiring for two AI engineer roles."

**Your comment:**
```
Good timing for hiring. Scaling team usually means eval infrastructure breaks at some point. Curious what your testing approach looks like as the team grows?
```

**Why it works:** You're not congratulating. You're showing you understand what hiring at scale means for infrastructure.

---

**Their post:** "Multi-model agents are the future, but testing is hard."

**Your comment:**
```
Harder than single-model because now you need to answer: which model is better for this task? And that answer changes based on the actual task. Most teams don't have a framework for testing that systematically.
```

**Why it works:** You're extending their thought with insight. You're not pitching. You're adding value to the conversation.

## DM Templates (Days 7–14 after connection)

*Use after 2–3 comment engagements. Only send DM if they've engaged with your comments or posted recently.*

### **Template A: Insight + Resource**
*Best for director-level and above*

```
Hey [Name],

Enjoyed your recent post on [topic]. Been thinking about the orchestration piece—found this guide on scaling evals across multi-agent systems. Might be useful.

[Link to one-pager or guide]

Would be interesting to hear how you're thinking about it.
```

**Why it works:** You're offering value (resource) without asking for anything. You're showing you follow their work. The ask is soft ("would be interesting to hear").

---

### **Template B: Research Question**
*Best for VP/Head of AI level*

```
[Name], quick question I'm genuinely curious about:

When you're orchestrating multiple agents, how do you measure whether the orchestration logic itself is working well? Or is that something you're still figuring out?

Asking because most teams I talk to are unsure how to test that layer.
```

**Why it works:** You're asking a real research question. You're not selling. You're implying that this is a problem most teams have (social proof through research).

---

### **Template C: Specific Observation**
*Best for people actively shipping*

```
Hey [Name],

Saw you just shipped v0.4. The [feature] update is interesting—that's a bigger surface area to test than v0.3. Curious how you approach test coverage on rollouts like that?
```

**Why it works:** You're showing you track their releases. You're asking about a real-world problem they just created for themselves. No pitch.

## Conversation Starters (Once They Reply)

**If they ask a question back:** Answer it directly and ask one follow-up question. Keep the conversation going.

**If they say "interested," suggest:** "Worth a 15-min call to walk through how this works? Or happy to just send you something to read first—your call."

**If they seem hesitant:** Offer low-friction next step: "No pressure. Just want to make sure we're on the same page about what matters for your setup. What's the biggest measurement challenge you're facing right now?"

## LinkedIn Engagement Rules

**Do:**
- Comment on posts related to AI/agents/engineering
- Ask real questions that show you understand their world
- Engage 2–3 times before sending DM
- Keep DMs short (2–3 sentences)
- Reference their specific work/achievements

**Don't:**
- Engage with political, personal, or unrelated posts
- Leave generic praise ("great post!")
- Send DM before building rapport through comments
- Pitch Braintrust in DMs before they ask about it
- Engage too frequently (1–2 times per week is plenty)
- Use corporate speak ("synergize," "leverage," etc.)

## Monthly Review

Track these metrics:
- New connections per week
- % of connections with engagement (comments)
- % of engagements that turn into DMs
- % of DMs that get responses
- Time from connection to conversation starter DM
- DMs that turn into meetings

**Goal ratios:**
- 20–30% of cold connections should get post engagement from you
- 30–40% of engagements should turn into DMs
- 30–50% of DMs should get responses
- Of those responses, 20–30% should lead to calls

---

## Red Lines

- Don't send generic connection request (no message)
- Don't immediately pitch in DM before they've engaged
- Don't engage with posts you haven't actually read
- Don't comment just to get visibility (it comes across as bot-like)
- Don't spam: max 2 connections per day, max 1 engagement per person per week
- Don't mention Braintrust until they ask about it
- Don't use LinkedIn like email (one-off messages without context)

---

## LinkedIn vs. Email Strategy

**LinkedIn is best for:**
- Warm-up and relationship building before other outreach
- Engaging with content (demonstrating expertise)
- Long-term relationship building

**Email is best for:**
- Direct cold outreach with specific value prop
- Structured sequences
- Clear CTA

**Most effective sequence:** LinkedIn engagement first (1–2 weeks) + then cold email (different angle/value prop) = higher response rate than either alone.
