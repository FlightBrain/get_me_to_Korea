# Outreach System Overview

## Purpose
Complete outreach playbook for Kensington, SDR at Braintrust. This system covers strategy, messaging, sequencing, and channel-specific approaches for building a predictable, defensible pipeline of AI-native companies.

## Quick Start (30 seconds)

1. **Find a target:** Use OUTREACH_STRATEGY.md ICP definition
2. **Research signals:** Look for hiring, funding, product launches (PERSONALIZATION_LIBRARY.md)
3. **Draft message:** Use OUTREACH_DRAFT_TEMPLATE.md
4. **Check messaging:** Review against MESSAGING_PRINCIPLES.md
5. **Send:** Log in appropriate channel index (EMAIL_INDEX.md, LINKEDIN_INDEX.md, etc.)
6. **Follow up:** Use FOLLOW_UP_SEQUENCES.md cadence

## The Four Phases of Outreach

### **Phase 1: Discovery (Research & Signal Hunting)**
*Goal: Find targets and understand their context*

Activities:
- Use OUTREACH_STRATEGY.md ICP to identify target companies
- Monitor hiring (LinkedIn, job boards, Reddit)
- Monitor funding (Crunchbase, TechCrunch, Twitter)
- Monitor product launches (company blogs, X, Reddit)
- Use PERSONALIZATION_LIBRARY.md to understand signal significance

Timeline: Ongoing

Output: List of 20–30 active targets with signals

---

### **Phase 2: Warm-Up (Build Relationship)**
*Goal: Get them to notice you before you ask for anything*

Activities:
- Engage on X/Twitter (quote tweets, replies, insights)
- Comment thoughtfully on Reddit (help first, no pitch)
- Connect on LinkedIn with personalized request
- Comment on their LinkedIn posts 2–3 times
- DM on LinkedIn with genuine insight (no CTA)

Timeline: 1–2 weeks per prospect

Output: Warm relationship, they know you exist and respect your perspective

---

### **Phase 3: Direct Outreach (Structured Sequence)**
*Goal: Start conversation with clear value prop*

Activities:
- Send cold email using FOLLOW_UP_SEQUENCES.md Touch 1
- Follow touches 2–5 on 5–7 day cadence
- Reference warm-up work if appropriate ("Saw your post on X")
- Move to conversation immediately if they reply (don't keep blasting sequence)
- After Touch 5, move to nurture if silence

Timeline: 2–4 weeks per prospect (5 touches)

Output: 15–25% response rate, some meetings scheduled

---

### **Phase 4: Nurture & Re-engagement**
*Goal: Stay in front of them without being pushy*

Activities:
- High-value content (no pitch): Articles, frameworks, case studies
- Monitor for new signals (hiring, funding, launches)
- Re-engage when signal changes their situation
- Use RE_ENGAGEMENT_SEQUENCES.md when timing is right
- Move to active sequence when they're warm again

Timeline: 30–60 days, ongoing

Output: Some prospects move back to active sequences when situation changes

## Positioning Reminders

**Lead with insight, not pitch.**
Every message should make them think "that's interesting" before they think "oh, this is a sales email."

**Three Braintrust Positioning Angles:**

1. **Execution Layer**
   - Evals aren't post-launch polish; they're part of dev velocity
   - Measurement lets you ship agent iterations faster
   - *Use when:* Prospect is iterating fast, scaling team, shipping new versions

2. **Vibes to Evals**
   - Intuition is your most valuable signal; we make it measurable
   - User feedback loops are data; structure them
   - *Use when:* Prospect has shipped but can't iterate systematically

3. **Reliability Through Measurement**
   - Every agent release is a risk release; quantify it
   - Measurement is how you ship with confidence
   - *Use when:* Prospect is regulated (healthcare, fintech) or customer-facing

## Weekly Cadence

### **Monday: Planning**
- Review previous week's responses and metrics
- Identify 3–5 new targets with fresh signals
- Plan warm-up activities (LinkedIn engagement, X comments)
- Draft cold emails for Touch 1 sends this week

### **Tuesday–Thursday: Execution**
- Send 3–5 cold emails (T1)
- Engage on X/Twitter (2–3 quality quote tweets or replies)
- Engage on Reddit (1–2 helpful responses)
- LinkedIn: Accept connections, engage on 2–3 posts
- Respond to all replies/questions within 24 hours

### **Friday: Follow-ups & Cleanup**
- Send scheduled follow-ups (Touches 2–5)
- Log all activity in channel indices
- Update prospect tracking sheet
- Respond to any end-of-week replies

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Generic personalization:** "I noticed you work in AI" is not personalization. Use PERSONALIZATION_LIBRARY.md.
2. **Pitching before asking:** Don't explain Braintrust until they ask about the problem.
3. **Following up too fast:** 5–7 days minimum between touches. 48-hour follow-ups feel desperate.
4. **Long emails:** Under 100 words for cold email. Period. Every word should earn its place.
5. **Mentioning competitors:** Never say "we're better than [tool]." Say "we solve this specific problem."
6. **Blasting sequences:** If they reply to T1, shift to conversation. Don't keep following the sequence.
7. **Corporate speak:** No "leveraging," "synergize," "unlock potential." Use human language.
8. **Ignoring silence:** Silence after T5 doesn't mean no. Move to nurture and re-engage on new signals.

## When to Escalate / Pivot

**Keep pushing if:**
- Company matches ICP (right size, right stage, right signals)
- Prospect is in relevant role (VP Eng, Head of AI, CTO)
- Signals suggest active agent development
- They've engaged at least once (reply, like, comment)

**Move to nurture if:**
- 5 touches with no response
- Soft rejection ("not a priority right now")
- Wrong person (junior engineer, not decision maker)
- Signal indicates they're not ready yet (too early stage, no hiring)

**Escalate to AE if:**
- They ask about pricing or ROI
- They want to talk to a customer
- They're asking detailed questions about implementation
- They've agreed to a meeting with strong interest signals

## Monthly Review Checklist

By the last Friday of each month:
1. Review response rates by touch, by channel, by industry
2. Identify top-performing personalization signals
3. Update PERSONALIZATION_LIBRARY.md with new hooks
4. Review objections from conversations—update OBJECTION_HANDLING.md if needed
5. Identify messaging that's working—call out what resonates
6. Identify messaging that's not—plan revisions
7. Report on metrics: emails sent, responses, meetings, stage of pipeline

## Final Mindset

**This is a volume + quality game.**

- Volume: You need to reach out to 20–30 prospects per month to get 4–8 meetings
- Quality: Every message should be personalized, every engagement should be authentic

**Sales is just helping people solve problems.**

- Lead with insight. Show you understand their world.
- Ask real questions. Find out if you can actually help.
- Be honest about fit. Don't waste their time if you can't help.

**Measurement matters.**

- Track everything. Metrics tell you what's working.
- Review weekly. Adjust based on what the data shows.
- Test and iterate. Your messaging gets better every month.

Good luck.
