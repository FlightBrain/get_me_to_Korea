# Outreach Strategy

## Purpose
Define the overall philosophy, positioning, and approach for all outreach activities. This document aligns messaging, channel selection, and engagement tactics around Braintrust's value proposition and Kensington's SDR priorities.

## How to Use
Reference this before launching any outreach campaign. Use the ICP definition to filter targets, messaging pillars to frame every conversation, and the engagement model to decide timing and channel sequencing.

---

## ICP Definition: The Execution-Measurement Gap

**Primary Persona:** VP Engineering, Head of AI, AI Platform Lead

**Company Profile:**
- Revenue: $10M–$500M+ (or Series B+ funding)
- Building AI agents or agentic workflows (internal tools, customer-facing products, or both)
- Actively hiring AI/ML engineers or shipping new AI products in the last 6–12 months
- Have shipped multiple agent iterations or versions
- Using LLM APIs (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.) at meaningful scale
- Pain point: "Our evals are manual spreadsheets, Slack threads, or we're not doing them systematically"

**Negative ICP:**
- Pure AI research teams (not building production systems)
- Companies only running LLM fine-tuning (no agents/orchestration)
- Startups under $2M revenue with zero prior funding
- Companies with no signs of AI hiring or product launches in the past 12 months

---

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. **Execution Layer**: Evals as Infrastructure
Position Braintrust as the *orchestration and observability layer* for agents—the same way observability companies own distributed tracing. Evals aren't a post-launch luxury; they're how you ship reliable agents faster.

**Key angle:** "You're already thinking about evals. We make that systematic, automated, and cross-tool."

### 2. **Vibes to Evals**: Translation Layer
Most engineers think in terms of "the agent didn't work" or "the LLM picked the wrong tool." Braintrust translates vibes (subjective feedback) into structured eval signals you can actually measure and iterate on.

**Key angle:** "Intuition is your most valuable signal. We make it measurable."

### 3. **Reliability Through Measurement**
Every agent iteration ships risk. Braintrust lets you quantify that risk in minutes—not weeks of manual testing. This is especially powerful for companies moving from prototype to production.

**Key angle:** "You can't improve what you don't measure. Measurement is how you ship with confidence."

## Channels & Sequencing

### **Primary (Full Cadence)**
- **Email:** Initial outreach + follow-up sequence
- **LinkedIn:** Connection + multi-touch engagement (comments, DMs)
- **X/Twitter:** Monitor and engage on public signals

### **Secondary (Research & Community)**
- **Reddit:** Monitor r/MachineLearning, r/LangChain, r/OpenAI for hiring signals
- **HN/Twitter:** Monitor for product launches or company announcements

### **Tertiary (Low-Effort Social)**
- Comment on relevant posts (no hard pitch, genuine insight)
- Retweet and add context to industry conversations

## Engagement Model: The Three-Phase Approach

### **Phase 1: Discovery (Weeks 1–2)**
- Research company's AI hiring, product launches, funding announcements
- Look for technical signals: GitHub repos, blog posts, X posts by engineering leadership
- Initial email or LinkedIn connection (clear value prop, not a pitch)
- Goal: Get response or engagement confirmation

### **Phase 2: Validation (Weeks 3–4)**
- Share specific insight tied to their situation (use Signal Library)
- Ask a research question ("How are you currently testing agent reliability?")
- Demo clip or one-pager if they show interest
- Goal: Understand their eval maturity and pain

### **Phase 3: Qualification (Weeks 5–8)**
- Position Braintrust as natural next step
- Intro to Customer Success if they're warm
- Reposition as "we help teams like [similar company] ship evals faster"
- Goal: Qualified conversation or nurture sequence

## Personalization Approach

**Level 1 (Minimum):** Use their name, reference their company by name, include one company-specific signal (hiring, product, funding).

**Level 2 (Standard):** Add a specific achievement or technical detail. Example: "Saw you shipped Claude integration in v0.3—evals are even more critical with multi-model orchestration."

**Level 3 (Premium):** Reference their personal content (blog post, tweet, GitHub contribution). Show you've done research on their specific tech stack or approach.

**Red Flag Personalization:** Generic "I noticed you work in AI" messages or fake generic insights. We only reach out if we have a specific signal.

---

## Tone & Positioning

- **Lead with expertise, not eagerness.** We're not desperate to sell; we're sharing what we know.
- **Use their language.** They say "observability," we say "observability." They say "testing," we match that.
- **Assume they're already doing evals.** Our angle is "here's how to do it better/faster/across tools."
- **No hype about AI.** We're practical infrastructure, not "leveraging the AI revolution."
- **Show shipping velocity.** Reference how fast teams using Braintrust iterate.

---

## Success Metrics

- **Response rate:** Target 15–25% for cold email (with strong personalization)
- **Meeting rate:** Target 5–10% of responses (qualification is a filter)
- **Sequence completion:** Track how many prospects finish 5-touch sequence before you pivot to nurture
- **Objection patterns:** Log recurring objections to feed back into messaging
