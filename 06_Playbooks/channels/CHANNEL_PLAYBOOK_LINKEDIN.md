# CHANNEL_PLAYBOOK_LINKEDIN.md

**Purpose:** Complete LinkedIn strategy. Connection approach, DM sequences, content engagement, research protocol.

**When to Use:** After email for warm follow-up, or when you have warm intro context. Also for profile research (LINKEDIN_RESEARCH.md) and engagement.

---

## When LinkedIn Works Best

- **Warm follow-up** (email sent, no response, try LinkedIn DM)
- **Warm intro available** (mutual connection can introduce)
- **High-engagement prospects** (active on LinkedIn, engaging on posts)
- **Relationship building** (before direct ask)
- **Executive level** (LinkedIn is office hours for some VPs)

When NOT to use LinkedIn:
- Complete cold (no mutual connection, no context)—email is better
- Trying to reach someone who's inactive on LinkedIn (last post 6+ months ago)
- Low-signal prospects (save time, focus on hot leads)

## Connection Request Approach

**Timing:** After email is sent (day 3-5), or with warm intro.

**Request message (if allowed):**
- With research: "Hi [Name], I saw your recent post on eval infrastructure and think a brief conversation could be valuable."
- With warm intro: "Hi [Name], [Mutual] suggested I reach out—I think you'd find our approach to scaling evals interesting."
- Generic (worst): No message, just request. Lower acceptance rate.

**Accept rate expectations:**
- With personalized message + mutual connection: 60-70%
- With personalized message, no mutual: 30-40%
- Generic request, no context: <10%

**Rule:** If you send connection request, send it WITH a message. No message = lower acceptance.

## DM Sequence (After They Accept)

**DM 1: Immediate (day of acceptance or next day)**
Goal: Start conversation, not pitch.

Example:
```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for accepting. I've been following your work on [specific thing they posted about]—really thoughtful on [specific insight].

I think you'd find interesting what we're doing with automated eval pipelines. Worth a quick chat sometime?

[First name]
```

Rules:
- One short message (2-3 sentences)
- Reference something specific they posted/did
- Offer low-commitment conversation
- No product talk yet

**DM 2: If no response (day 3-4)**
Goal: Add new context, not repeat.

Example:
```
Also—just saw [Company] is hiring for [Role]. Signals you're ramping up eval infrastructure. Might be a good time to talk?
```

Rules:
- Only if new signal appeared (they posted, hired, got funding, changed jobs)
- Not "just checking in"
- Make it about THEM, not you

**DM 3: If still no response (day 7)**
Goal: Graceful exit, keep door open.

Example:
```
No worries if timing isn't right. If things shift on your roadmap with evals, definitely reach out.
```

Rules:
- Respectful, not pushy
- This is last DM in sequence
- Goal: not burn bridge

**Rule:** Don't DM more than 3 times per contact without new signal. Feels desperate.

## Content Engagement Strategy

**Goal:** Build relationship, show expertise, get on their radar before DM.

**Best engagement tactics:**

1. **Comment on their posts (thoughtfully)**
   - Their post: "Scaling eval at [company]—challenging"
   - Your comment: "Have you noticed [specific challenge] as you scale? We solved this by [insight]." (not a pitch, just insight)
   - Goal: They see your comment, notice you understand the pain, become more receptive to DM

2. **React to posts** (less effective but low-lift)
   - React with thumbs up, celebrate emoji if relevant
   - Increases visibility but less impactful than comment

3. **Share their content** (with your own take)
   - If they write something smart about eval, share with comment: "This resonates—here's what we're seeing at [company]: [insight]"
   - They get notified, see you're thinking about same problems

**Timing:** Engage 2-3 times before sending DM. Establishes rapport without being pushy.

## Profile Research Protocol

Use this before reaching out. (See LINKEDIN_RESEARCH.md for full playbook)

Check before DM:
- [ ] Is their title aligned with our buyer? (VP ML, not HR)
- [ ] Recent post? (Last 30 days = more likely active, responsive)
- [ ] What are they posting about? (Is it relevant to eval, reliability, AI?)
- [ ] Mutual connections? (How warm is intro?)
- [ ] Company signals? (Hiring, funding, growth)

If profile shows:
- Recent activity + relevant pain = high priority for DM
- No activity for 6+ months = lower priority, maybe skip LinkedIn
- Right company but not the buyer = research who the real buyer is, don't waste time

## Complete Outreach Sequence

1. **Day 1: Email (cold)**
   - Cold email with research-backed hook

2. **Day 2: LinkedIn Research**
   - Check their profile, posts, signals
   - Note engagement level

3. **Day 4: LinkedIn Engagement**
   - Comment on their recent post (if relevant)
   - Like/react to show you follow them

4. **Day 5: Connection Request**
   - Send request with personalized message
   - "Hi [Name], I saw your post on [X]—think you'd find [Y] valuable. Open to a quick chat?"

5. **Day 5-6: (After acceptance) First DM**
   - Reference what you engaged on
   - Offer low-friction conversation

6. **Day 8: LinkedIn Follow-up** (if no DM response)
   - New signal if available: "Also saw you're hiring for [Role]..."
   - Or: graceful exit

**Overall timing:** Email → LinkedIn takes 1 week. If both get no response, archive for 3 months.

## Common Mistakes

- **Generic connection request** — No message, no personalization. Acceptance rate tanks.
- **Pitching in first DM** — "Check out our product!" = they unmatch. Lead with insight, not pitch.
- **DMing before emailing** — Harder to reach someone cold on LinkedIn. Email first for credibility.
- **Not researching before DM** — Send DM without knowing they're active on LinkedIn. No response = they don't check it.
- **Spamming content** — Like 10 posts in a row = looks like bot. Engage with 2-3 most relevant posts, stop.
- **Following up too often** — 4+ DMs without new signal = desperate. Max 3 DMs per sequence.
- **Accepting every connection** — Stay selective. Accept connections from people in your target market. Others are noise.

## Maintenance Notes

- **LinkedIn algorithm:** Engagement (likes, comments) helps your content show to your network. Post 1x/week for visibility.
- **Batch connection requests:** Sunday evening, send 5-10 connection requests with messages. Better acceptance rates.
- **Seasonal:** Q2-Q3 = more cold outreach. Q4 = more warm follow-up of existing convos.
- **Profile strength:** Keep your profile updated. If someone researches you back, you look credible.
- **Privacy:** Be mindful of LinkedIn scraping rules. Don't automate DMs. Manual outreach only.
- **Content strategy:** Share 1-2 insights per month on eval, reliability, LLM topics. Builds credibility.
