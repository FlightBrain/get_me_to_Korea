# LINKEDIN_RESEARCH.md

**Purpose:** Research a prospect's profile, context, and signals before outreach. Gather intelligence that makes your message personal and relevant.

**When to Use:** Before every cold email or connection request. 5-10 min per prospect.

**Inputs Needed:**
- Prospect name (from company research or signal)
- LinkedIn account (logged in)
- Company name and industry
- SIGNALS.md (to cross-reference)

---

## Steps

1. **Check Profile Basics** (1 min)
   - Job title: Is this the right person? (VP ML, not HR Manager)
   - Current company: Match target company
   - How long at current company: <1 year = maybe new role, good signal
   - Location: Relevant for timezone, regional focus

2. **Scan Recent Posts & Activity** (2 min)
   - Have they posted in last 30 days? (Activity = likely to check LinkedIn)
   - What are they posting about? (technical, recruiting, product launches)
   - Any posts about: LLMs, evaluation, reliability, AI, models? (confirms pain relevance)
   - Example: VP Engineering posted "Scaling our ML evaluation pipeline" 2 weeks ago = HOT signal
   - File: Note what they're discussing in SIGNALS.md

3. **Review Company Page** (2 min)
   - Industry + size: Who you're targeting? (startup, enterprise, VC-backed)
   - Recent hiring: "Hiring for: ML Engineer, ML Ops, Reliability Engineer" = pain area signal
   - Recent posts by company: Are they discussing AI, evaluation, models?
   - Company description: Confirms product/use case
   - Example: "Raising Series B" + "hiring ML Ops" = growth mode with eval needs

4. **Look at Job Postings** (1 min)
   - Click "Company careers" tab if available
   - What roles are open? "Evaluation engineer," "ML reliability engineer" = eval maturity gap
   - Job descriptions mention what? ("Improve model reliability," "test LLM outputs") = specific pain
   - File: Note in SIGNALS.md as "job posting signal"

5. **Check Mutual Connections** (1 min)
   - Any shared contacts? (Can you get warm intro?)
   - Are they connected to known advocates or users?
   - If warm connection exists: mention in email ("I see you're connected to [Name] at Braintrust")

6. **Review Engagement Patterns** (1 min)
   - Do they comment on posts? (Engaged, likely to respond)
   - Do they endorse skills? (Organized, takes LinkedIn seriously)
   - Join date on LinkedIn: Active? Dormant? (Old account, maybe not checking LinkedIn)
   - Timing note: High engagement = better chance of response

## What to Capture & Where to File It

| Signal | File Location | Example |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| Recent post about pain | SIGNALS.md | "Sarah Chen posted on 'hallucination testing' last week" |
| Hiring signals | SIGNALS.md | "VP Engineering - Role open for ML Ops (eval gap)" |
| Career timeline | Email draft | "Recently promoted to VP ML (6 months ago)" → signals ambition/authority |
| Company news | SIGNALS.md | "Company raising Series B" + "recruiting heavily" |
| Technical expertise | Email draft | Use in personalization: "Your work on RLHF alignment is..." |
| Pain language in posts | CUSTOMER_LANGUAGE.md | Copy exact phrase from post if relevant |
| Mutual connection | Email draft | Mention in opener: "I notice you know [person] at..." |
| Engagement level | Decision on channel | High engagement = try LinkedIn DM. Low = stick with email |

## Common Mistakes

- **Wrong decision-maker** — Message a junior engineer when you need the VP ML. They're not signing off on deals.
- **No post/profile awareness** — Send generic email without referencing their recent activity. Feels cold even on warm channel.
- **Not checking if they're actively using LinkedIn** — Last post from 2023. They won't see your DM. Use email instead.
- **Ignoring hiring signals** — They posted "hiring for ML evaluation engineer." That's YOU. Mention it in outreach.
- **Not filing insights** — You research 10 people, forget which ones had relevant posts. Insights don't compound.
- **Overthinking research** — Spend 15 min on one person when 5 min gets 95% of useful intel. Speed > perfect.
- **Not confirming company/pain match** — Research says they work in AI, but their LinkedIn shows no product AI usage. Misaligned target.

## Maintenance Notes

- **Time-box to 5-10 min** — If you're spending 20 min per prospect, you're overthinking. Skim posts, capture signal, move.
- **Use Chrome extensions** — Tools like Hunter, Apollo can auto-pull contact info from LinkedIn. Saves copying manually.
- **Friday research day** — During WEEKLY_REVIEW.md, research 10-15 high-priority prospects at once. Batch > context-switch.
- **Update SIGNALS.md incrementally** — After each research session, add signals found. They inform next week's outreach.
- **Seasonal hiring patterns** — Q1/Q2 heavy hiring (growth season). Q3 slower. Plan research volume accordingly.
- **Quarterly check-ins** — Re-research hot prospects every 90 days. Job changed? Company trajectory shifted? Update intel.
