# DAILY_SHUTDOWN.md

**Purpose:** Capture the day's work, clean up loose ends, and set up tomorrow for success in 10 minutes. Lock in progress so nothing falls through cracks.

**When to Use:** Every evening, 15-30 min before logging off.

**Inputs Needed:**
- Today's outreach list (what you sent)
- Email drafts or templates used
- Conversations with prospects
- New blockers or insights discovered
- Tomorrow's prep from TODAY.md

---

## Steps (10 minutes)

1. **Update DONE_LOG.md** (2 min)
   - Add all completed tasks from today
   - For outreach: note count sent, channels used, response rate if known
   - Example: "Sent 12 personalized emails to AI safety researchers. 2 opens within 2 hours."
   - Log wins and progress (even small ones build momentum)

2. **Move Incomplete Tasks** (1 min)
   - Review your 3 priorities—which did you finish?
   - Move unfinished tasks to ACTIVE_CONTEXT.md or backlog
   - Add any new tasks discovered today to inbox (don't process, just capture)

3. **Update WAITING_ON.md** (1 min)
   - Add new items you're waiting on: follow-ups, decisions, bounces
   - Example: "Sarah Chen—waiting on final pricing approval, due Monday"
   - Flag if anything needs escalation or reminder

4. **Capture Insights & New Preferences** (2 min)
   - If you discovered new pain language, add to CUSTOMER_LANGUAGE.md
   - If you found good signals, log to SIGNALS.md
   - If you learned something about your messaging that works, update MESSAGING.md
   - Example: "Openers mentioning 'hallucination' get 3x response rate"

5. **Write Session Log Entry** (1 min)
   - One sentence: what moved the needle today?
   - Example: "Closed AI safety researcher campaign. 18 outreaches, 3 meetings booked. Pain language updated."
   - Include: outreach volume, meetings booked, deals moved

6. **Prep Tomorrow's TODAY.md** (2 min)
   - Write 3 priorities for tomorrow (use DAILY_STARTUP.md format)
   - Flag any follow-ups due tomorrow
   - Add any time-sensitive context (e.g., "Sarah Chen needs pricing by EOD Tuesday")
   - Set one specific win you want tomorrow (e.g., "Book 1 discovery call")

## Examples

**Today's work:** Sent 15 emails, booked 1 call, discovered Sarah Chen is a hot signal, got stuck waiting on Marcus from Stripe.

**DONE_LOG entry:**
```
[April 1] Sent 15 personalized outreaches to AI safety researchers. Opened 4. Booked 1 discovery call with Sarah Chen (Anthropic). Noticed "hallucination testing" language appears in 6 replies.
```

**WAITING_ON entry:**
```
Sarah Chen — Pricing deck pending. She asked for comparative pricing vs. Anthropic's internal evals. Due: Tuesday EOD.
Marcus (Stripe ML) — PTO until April 7, following up then with new use case angle.
```

**CUSTOMER_LANGUAGE update:**
```
New high-signal phrases:
- "hallucination testing" (6 mentions in 1 day)
- "eval latency" (3 mentions, ML ops focused)

```

**Tomorrow's TODAY.md:**
```
=== TODAY — April 2 ===

CONTEXT:
- Sarah Chen responsive on pricing
- AI safety researcher campaign hot
- Need to close Stripe angle before Marcus returns

PRIORITIES:
1. Finalize pricing deck for Sarah Chen (1 hour, morning)
2. Send 12 more outreaches to ML ops leads using "eval latency" angle (2 hours, 10-12)
3. Draft follow-up sequence for opened emails (30 min)

WIN: Book 1 more discovery call by EOD
```

## Common Mistakes

- **Not logging wins** — You sent 12 emails, 1 opened, but didn't capture it. Next week you wonder if outreach is working. Always log numbers.
- **Adding too many tasks to Tomorrow** — Tomorrow becomes impossible, you get discouraged. Stick to 3 priorities.
- **Forgetting WAITING_ON** — You follow up with Marcus without realizing he's on PTO. Wastes the follow-up. Check WAITING_ON in startup.
- **Not updating CUSTOMER_LANGUAGE** — You find that "hallucination" gets great response but don't document it. Next month you've forgotten why it worked.
- **Skipping session logs** — You can't remember what happened 2 weeks ago when reviewing performance. Logs are your sales history.
- **Prepping tomorrow while tired** — Last-minute planning is fuzzy. Do it 15 min before end of day when you're sharp.

## Output Format

Keep this template in your daily work area:

```
=== DAILY SHUTDOWN — [DATE] ===

WORK COMPLETED:
- [task] — [outcome/notes]
- [outreach] — [count, channel, response rate]
- [call/meeting] — [notes, next steps]

DONE_LOG ENTRY (copy to file):
[Date] [Summary of work: outreaches sent, calls booked, deals moved]

WAITING_ON (new items):
- [prospect/task] — [what you're waiting for, due date]

NEW INSIGHTS CAPTURED:
- CUSTOMER_LANGUAGE: [pain phrases, if any]
- SIGNALS: [new signals discovered]
- MESSAGING: [what worked, what didn't]

TOMORROW'S PRIORITIES (pre-write in TODAY.md):
1. [priority] — [time estimate]
2. [priority] — [time estimate]
3. [priority] — [time estimate]

SESSION WIN:
[One outcome you're proud of today]
```

## Maintenance Notes

- **Timing:** Do shutdown 15-30 min before you want to leave. Rushing loses the insights.
- **Memory updates:** Spend 30 sec on these. They compound over time and become your playbook.
- **Weekly cadence:** Friday shutdown is longer—use it to prep WEEKLY_REVIEW.md (see that playbook).
- **Pattern spotting:** After 2-3 weeks, review your logs. Are there patterns in what's working? Update your playbooks.
- **Ritual:** Make this automatic, like brushing teeth. You'll never lose a win or blocker again.
