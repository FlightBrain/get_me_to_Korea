# DAILY_STARTUP.md

**Purpose:** Establish context and priorities for the day in 15 minutes. Know what matters, what's blocking you, and what wins you're chasing.

**When to Use:** First thing every morning before checking Slack or email.

**Inputs Needed:**
- ACTIVE_CONTEXT.md (current priorities, ongoing deals)
- TODAY.md (tasks and focus from yesterday's shutdown)
- WAITING_ON.md (tasks blocked on others)
- DONE_LOG.md (recent wins for momentum)

---

## Steps (15 minutes)

1. **Review ACTIVE_CONTEXT.md** (2 min)
   - Remind yourself of current campaigns and high-priority prospects
   - Note any VIPs you're chasing this quarter
   - Flag any urgent blockers from yesterday

2. **Check Calendar** (2 min)
   - Scan your calendar for today's meetings
   - Identify time blocks for outreach (usually 10-11 AM, 2-3 PM)
   - Note any demos, calls, or internal meetings

3. **Read TODAY.md from shutdown** (2 min)
   - See what you identified as today's focus last night
   - Confirm it's still the right priority
   - Adjust if priorities shifted overnight (check Slack/email subject lines)

4. **Scan WAITING_ON.md** (2 min)
   - Check if any blockers have been resolved
   - Move resolved items to DONE_LOG.md
   - Flag if any WAITING_ON items are now critical path

5. **Check Inbox (email & Slack)** (3 min)
   - Quick scan for urgent messages (replies, blocker resolutions, new opportunities)
   - Capture any new work into inbox, don't process yet
   - Note: Don't get sucked into Slack threads—batch reply later

6. **Update ACTIVE_CONTEXT.md** (1 min)
   - Add any new signals/opportunities from overnight
   - Confirm current working set still accurate
   - Mark if any deals moved (closed, lost, stalled)

7. **Pick Top 3 Priorities** (1 min)
   - Write down your 3 main wins for today
   - Example: "Close 1 decision-maker interview," "Send 12 personalized outreaches," "Finalize case study quote"
   - Time-box each (15 outreaches = 2 hours, 1 call = 1.5 hours, etc.)

## Examples

**Context:** Monday, April 1. You're running 3 concurrent campaigns (AI eval startups, ML ops teams, VCs).

**ACTIVE_CONTEXT.md shows:**
- Campaign: "Eval-focused AI startups" — 14 prospects in pipeline
- Hot: Sarah Chen (Anthropic safety team) — responsive, waiting on pricing
- Blocked: Marcus (Stripe ML) — on PTO until April 7

**Today.md from shutdown:**
- Personalize 8 more outreaches to "AI safety research leads"
- Follow up with Sarah Chen on pricing question
- Prepare 2 demo slides for Thursday calls

**Startup decision:**
- **Priority 1:** Draft + send 8 outreaches to AI safety researchers (2 hrs, 10-12 PM)
- **Priority 2:** Send Sarah Chen pricing comparison email (15 min, 12:15 PM)
- **Priority 3:** Build demo slides (1 hr, 3-4 PM)
- **Buffer:** LinkedIn research on 2 new prospects if hitting all three

---

## Common Mistakes

- **Not reading ACTIVE_CONTEXT.md** — Start meeting someone new without context of where they are in pipeline. Result: wasted time, unfocused follow-up.
- **Skipping TODAY.md** — You replan from scratch every morning instead of building momentum. Leads to decision fatigue.
- **Getting stuck in Slack** — Saw a message, responded to 5 threads, now it's 11 AM. Guard your first 2 hours ruthlessly.
- **Picking 8 priorities instead of 3** — You execute on surface-level, hit none deeply. Be ruthless about what actually moves the needle.
- **Not time-boxing** — Say "do outreach" but don't know it takes 2 hrs. Run out of time, start slow.

## Output Format

Copy this to your daily work document at startup:

```
=== DAILY STARTUP — [DATE] ===

CONTEXT:
- [1-2 sentence refresh of what you're working on]
- Active campaigns: [list]
- VIP prospects: [1-2 hot names]

BLOCKERS TO RESOLVE:
- [any waiting_on items that matter today]

TOP 3 PRIORITIES:
1. [priority] — [time estimate, window]
2. [priority] — [time estimate, window]
3. [priority] — [time estimate, window]

INBOX CAPTURES (process in shutdown):
- [new opportunity, new blocker, etc.]
```

---

## Maintenance Notes

- **When to revise:** If you're not hitting your 3 priorities regularly, startup isn't setting realistic goals or you're not time-boxing. Adjust in DAILY_SHUTDOWN.md
- **Seasonal:** On Mondays, plan the week too (30 sec quick addition)
- **Slack integration:** Consider a 9 AM reminder in Slack to run through this (or do it before opening Slack)
- **Time-boxing is key:** If you plan 12 things, you'll do 2. Pick 3 you can actually complete.
