## What Works

*Patterns that have led to wins, efficiency, or insight. Reference these when planning.*

### Prospect Research
- **Warm intros are the SDR's job to source.** Never ask the AE "who do you have warm connections at?" Research the AE's LinkedIn connections, previous companies (e.g., Walton was at Athenahealth), and mutual connections. Present warm intro paths as findings in the 1:1, not questions. The SDR's job is pipeline generation: putting PG work on the AE is a failure mode.

### Messaging
- **Winning email format for 1-off outbound:** (1) Their recent AI/GenAI release or signal (shows homework), (2) 1-line relevant case study matched to industry/role, (3) Link to braintrust.dev/customers (let the page sell), (4) Discovery question about pain point (how they're doing evals, biggest challenge, what's broken), (5) "Best," + HTML signature. The disco question IS the CTA, not "want to chat?"
- **Every email must be uniquely worded.** No two emails should read the same, even within the same account. Vary openers, transitions, case study framing, and question phrasing.
- **Subject lines:** Internal/casual, not salesy (e.g. "the supervisor routing problem" not "Braintrust for Nintex"). Lowercase. Under 100 words body. No em dashes ever.

### Bryan Cox Ghost Notes
- **Bryan's only explicit feedback ever:** "you might want to share more about the logos we work with or how we are solving the pain?" (HackerOne, March 2026). Logos + pain = mandatory in every note.
- **When Bryan fires off "launched" / "sent" same day:** the note had (a) specific signal in line 1, (b) matched logos, (c) pain tied to their role, (d) low-friction CTA. Both the Slingshot note and Jay's RKO dartboard batch triggered immediate sends.
- **Bryan's self-intro lines:** "I lead worldwide sales at Braintrust" (when warm bridge exists) or "I'm Bryan and lead the GTM team at Braintrust" (cold/event).
- **CTA language that works:** "swap notes on how you're doing this today" / "Are you open to meeting us there?" NOT "schedule a demo" or "start a trial."
- **No bullets in cold DMs.** Bullets only in event briefing format (Pattern 2 in ghost-notes.md). Cold DMs should read conversational.
- **Logo matching matters.** Don't give the same 10 logos to everyone. Match to their industry: fintech gets Stripe/Ramp/Robinhood, media gets Netflix/Spotify, enterprise gets Microsoft/Notion/Zapier.
- **Re-engagement notes:** Jay's explicit guidance: "less about 'lets go to POC' and more around prioritization of this project." Remove deal pressure, add prioritization question.
- **Sender routing:** Bryan defers to Drew Boisen on who should send (Bryan vs PB vs Ameya vs Drew). When unsure, flag it and let Drew decide.
- **Template vs. personalization trap:** The VIP Blitz batch had 8 notes that were too formulaic (only line 1 personalized). The personalization in line 1 MUST be specific to the actual AI initiative, not just "[Company]'s AI work."

## What Doesn't Work

*Approaches that have failed or wasted time. Avoid these.*

### Prospecting
- **Never name-drop internal contacts in subject lines.** Body is fine for referencing colleagues we've spoken with, but putting "karthik and chad's team" as a subject is too aggressive from an outside rep.
- **Check contact location before inviting to local events.** Hennie Engelbrecht is in South Africa, not Seattle. Always verify geography before including event invites.
- **Reference people we've spoken with at their company in the body** when we have that intel (e.g. "Vinit Tople has been in conversations with us, Ankita Sayal spun up a Braintrust org"). Creates urgency and social proof.
- **Name-drop customers in their line of work**, not just generic case studies. Match case study to their industry/role.
- **All emails are from Kensington**, not ghost notes. Even VP-level emails come from Kensington unless explicitly told otherwise.
- **Stagger sends 20-30/day** to protect email reputation. Never blast all at once.
- **Pattern-match emails** for contacts without verified addresses (firstname.lastname@ or firstinitial+lastname@ based on verified emails at the same company). Flag these as guesses.
- **Skip contacts with bad emails** (personal gmail, old company domains like chorus.ai or skuid.com or berkeley.edu).

## Rules of Thumb

*Heuristics that have proven reliable. Use when you're uncertain.*

1. **Research investment:** Spend 15-30 min per prospect before outreach. More yields diminishing returns; less feels generic.

2. **Response window:** If no reply in 5 days, one follow-up. If no reply after that, move on. Obsessing signals low confidence.

3. **Discovery structure:** Always ask: What are you trying to build? How is it breaking today? Who else is involved? Then listen.

4. **Proposal timing:** Only propose if you understand their problem better than they do. Otherwise, it's premature.

5. **Deal assessment:** If you're uncertain if they should buy, they shouldn't buy. Trust your gut on fit.

6. **Async-first:** Default to async. Sync only when information density or relationship-building requires it.

7. **CRM discipline:** Log everything same day. Tomorrow-you won't remember the details.

8. **Quota pacing:** Track weekly progress vs. linear quota. If behind after week 2, escalate immediately.

9. **DM recaps: action items only.** When recapping Slack DMs (daily or on-demand), only pull out action items and to-dos assigned to Kensington. No conversation summaries or narrative. Search DMs AND territory channels (#dave-smith-territory, #walton-stephens-territory, #jay-vermont-territory) and broader channels (#sales, #pg). Format as checklist items with context (who asked, when, account name, contact links). Update both Action Items and Agenda sections on each AE's Notion meeting notes page.

### Apollo & Enrichment
- **Pattern matching fills 2x more emails than Apollo enrichment alone** (152 vs 73 in Phase 1). Always look at existing emails at the same company first, identify the format, then generate.
- **Common email patterns by company:** first.last@ (most common: Wells Fargo, ZoomInfo, Nordstrom, TikTok, Dexcom, AMD), flast@ (Snap, Expedia, Barracuda, Plaid, Cloudera, Cerebras), first@ (small companies like Gremlin, Otter.ai, Sunbit, Robust Intelligence)
- **Block/Square have inconsistent email patterns** — can't reliably pattern match. Skip these for automated enrichment.
- **Grab has dual domains** (@grab.com vs @grabtaxi.com) — skip automated enrichment.
- **Always check for job changes** before applying pattern-matched emails. Apollo flags these. ~10-15% of contacts have left their company.
- **Apollo bulk match is limited to 10 per call** — batch accordingly. Only charges credits for matches.
- **"Other SDR Prospected" contacts exist in most accounts** — previous SDRs added people to Apollo. Search by company name, filter Director+ with sequence history, label them for re-engagement.
- **Use Sonnet model for mechanical Apollo tasks** (search, pattern match, update). Save Opus for research/planning.

## Anti-Patterns

*Red flags that indicate you're off-track.*

- **Feeling busy without pipeline growth:** You're doing activity, not strategy
- **Lots of meetings, few proposals:** Discovery isn't converting to conviction
- **High proposal volume, low close rate:** Your research or qualification is weak
- **Prospects ghosting after initial conversation:** Your messaging or discovery didn't resonate
- **Cycling through prospecting tactics weekly:** You're not giving strategies time to compound
