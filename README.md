# Kensington's Personal Operating System

Welcome to your AI-native workspace. This is a file-based operating system designed to be your persistent memory, task engine, and strategic command center for everything you do at Braintrust and beyond.

## What This Is

This workspace is built on a simple premise: **your brain is for thinking, not remembering**. Everything gets filed away here — context, decisions, customer intelligence, playbooks, templates, notes, and logs. Claude and other AI tools use this as your extended memory, so you never have to repeat context or rebuild tribal knowledge.

It's:
- **Markdown-first**: everything is plain text and readable
- **File-based memory**: no proprietary formats, just clean structure
- **Modular and searchable**: organized by numbered folders (00-99) that you can navigate quickly
- **Designed for AI collaboration**: Claude reads and updates files as your assistant, so you stay in control
- **Opinionated**: strong conventions about what goes where and how to organize it

## How It's Organized

The workspace uses **numbered folders (00-99)** for semantic organization. Each folder owns a domain:

### Top-Level Folders

| Folder | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| `00_inbox` | Triage zone — raw notes, quick captures, temporary files |
| `01_core_context` | Your North Star — role, goals, values, non-negotiables, operating system |
| `02_customers` | Customer intel, account profiles, deal status, playbooks specific to key accounts |
| `03_outbound` | Prospect research, cold sequences, messaging frameworks, templates |
| `04_sales_process` | Sales methodology, qualification criteria, deal stages, closing playbooks |
| `05_tools_systems` | How-to guides for Apollo, Slack, Gmail, Notion, Granola, LinkedIn — your operational toolkit |
| `06_meetings_calls` | Call recordings/notes (via Granola), meeting prep templates, post-call summaries |
| `07_learning` | Articles, books, frameworks you're internalizing, skill development tracking |
| `08_templates` | Email sequences, call scripts, pitch decks, qualification frameworks — reusable IP |
| `09_logs` | Daily standups, weekly reviews, quarterly OKRs, performance tracking |
| `10-98` | Available for expansion as your operating system grows |
| `99_archive` | Completed deals, old playbooks, historical context you might reference but don't actively use |

## Quick Start (First 5 Things)

1. **Read `01_core_context/your_role.md`** — Understand what you're optimizing for as an SDR at Braintrust.
2. **Review `02_customers/key_accounts.md`** — Know your current targets and context.
3. **Skim `08_templates/`** — See what templates are already prepped so you don't reinvent wheels.
4. **Check `05_tools_systems/`** — Know how to integrate Apollo, Granola, and Notion with your workflow.
5. **Set a weekly review habit** — Every Friday, spend 15 min updating `09_logs/weekly_review.md` so Claude has fresh context.

## Philosophy

This workspace exists to:
- **Eliminate context switching** — Everything you need is here, indexed and searchable
- **Create institutional memory** — Your customer intelligence, playbooks, and lessons learned don't disappear
- **Enable AI partnership** — Claude can read your memory, update it, and suggest next steps without you having to re-explain context
- **Save time** — No more "remind me of..." or "where was that email about...?" — it's all here
- **Compound learning** — Every interaction, every rejection, every win gets filed and analyzed for patterns

## How Claude Uses This

When you ask Claude for help, it:
1. Reads relevant sections of your memory to understand context
2. Executes tasks (research, drafting, analysis) in the workspace
3. Updates memory with the work done, decisions made, and next actions
4. Suggests what to do next
5. Always leaves an audit trail so you know what happened

You control what gets filed, reviewed, and archived. Claude is your executive assistant, not your dictator.

## Getting Started Now

Start by opening the files referenced in the Quick Start section. Then create your first daily entry in `09_logs/daily_standup.md` for today. Claude will help you populate the rest.
