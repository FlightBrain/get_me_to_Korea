## Examples

[Real examples of this process in action, showing what success looks like.]

**Example 1: SaaS Company (100-500 employees)**
- Discovery call: 45 min, strong fit on pain points
- Outputs: Technical overview (15 min), demo proposal (10 min)
- Result: Scheduled technical demo for 7 days later, 100% of technical questions answered before demo
- Outcome: 3-week sales cycle to pilot

**Example 2: Enterprise Company (1000+ employees)**
- Discovery call: 60 min, multiple stakeholders
- Outputs: Technical overview (30 min), compliance questionnaire (20 min)
- Result: Demo + compliance review scheduled 2 weeks out
- Outcome: 12-week sales cycle (expected for enterprise)

**Example 3: When It Went Wrong**
- Discovery call: 30 min, seemed interested but vague
- Mistake: Didn't confirm budget or decision timeline before sending technical overview
- Result: Technical overview ignored for 3 weeks, never got follow-up meeting
- Learning: Always ask explicit questions: "Do you have budget allocated?" "Who needs to approve?"

## Common Mistakes

[What do people often get wrong with this process? How to avoid it?]

- **Mistake 1:** [Description of common error]
  - **Why it happens:** [Root cause]
  - **How to avoid:** [Specific check or reminder]
  - **Impact if ignored:** [What goes wrong]

Example:

- **Mistake 1:** Sending technical overview before confirming technical contact
  - **Why it happens:** Eager to keep momentum, assume discovery person will share
  - **How to avoid:** Explicitly ask in discovery: "Who from your technical team should I send this to?" Get email before ending call
  - **Impact if ignored:** Document gets lost, no response, momentum dies

- **Mistake 2:** Using generic technical overview instead of customizing for their use case
  - **Why it happens:** Saves time, think generic is good enough
  - **How to avoid:** Always customize: mention their specific pain points, add proof point from their industry
  - **Impact if ignored:** Feels like form letter, lower response rate, perceived as low effort

- **Mistake 3:** Not following up if no response within 5 days
  - **Why it happens:** Assume they're busy, don't want to seem pushy
  - **How to avoid:** Add calendar reminder for day 5, send follow-up email with one new piece of value (e.g., relevant case study)
  - **Impact if ignored:** Deal stalls without reason, hard to recover
