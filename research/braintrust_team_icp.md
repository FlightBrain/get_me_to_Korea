## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Definition

### Company Archetypes (from Notion ICP doc + Slack)
1. **AI Companies** -- Companies where AI IS the product (e.g., Lovable, Ambience Healthcare, Mistral AI)
2. **Digital Natives** -- Tech-forward companies using AI in their products (e.g., Neo4j, Airbnb, Vercel, Notion, Stripe)
3. **Enterprises with AI Mandates** -- Large orgs with executive-level AI transformation initiatives (e.g., Microsoft, Expedia, NYTimes)

### Target Personas (by priority)

#### Engineers (Primary buyers/evaluators)
- **Staff+ AI Engineers** (e.g., Anji Boddupally @ Writer)
- **Directors to VPs of AI** (e.g., Sarah Sachs @ Notion)
- **Directors to VPs of Engineering** (e.g., Mohsen Sardari @ BILL)
- **Care most about:** Catching regressions, debugging production
- **Language to use:** CI/CD gates, model and framework-agnostic, test in parallel

#### Enterprise Platform Leaders
- **Sr. Director+ Platform/Engineering**
- **Director to VP of Platforms** (e.g., Hisham @ Expedia)
- **Director to VP of R&D** (e.g., Scott Lowenstein @ NYT)
- **Care most about:** Scaling teams, reducing risk
- **Language to use:** AI SDLC, compliance, operational excellence

#### Product Managers
- **Staff Product Managers** (e.g., Adrianna Baker @ Babylist)
- **Senior AI Product Managers** (e.g., Gary Wang @ Zoom)
- **Directors to VPs of Product** (e.g., Thu Nguyen @ 6Sense)
- **Care most about:** Making vibes measurable, shipping fast
- **Language to use:** No code, executive dashboards, collaborate with eng

### Company Qualification Thresholds (MQL Criteria from Notion)
- **Employee count >= 100** OR
- **Total funding >= $5M** OR
- **AI/ML role count >= 5 employees**
- Must have a work email or "enrich-able" profile
- Must have taken a marketing action (form, webinar, event, demo request, sign-up)

### Account Scoring Dimensions (from Salesforce/Clay enrichment)
Accounts are scored on a weighted rubric across these dimensions:
- **Industry Fit Score** (1-5): Core / General / Watchlist
- **AI Adoption/Maturity Score** (1-5): AI Critical / AI Enabled / etc.
- **AI Role Intensity Score** (0-5): Number of AI/ML roles relative to company size
- **Hiring Growth Score** (1-5): Downturn / Steady / Growing
- **Weighted Account Score** (composite): Overall priority score
- **Website AI Keywords**: Generative AI, AI-powered, scoring, chatbot, agentic, etc.
- **EE Growth (Past 6 Months)**: Employee growth percentage

### Account Tiering (used in blitzes and prospecting)
- **Tier A / Tier 1**: AI-Native / GenAI Products companies -- highest priority
- **Tier B / Tier 2**: Tech companies investing heavily in AI
- **Tier C**: Enterprise AI Initiatives -- large but slower-moving
- **Tier D**: Low ICP Fit -- deprioritize

### Excluded Titles (from MQL Criteria)
These job titles are auto-excluded from MQL qualification:
- Student, intern, undergrad
- Sales, AE, BDR, marketing, demand gen, recruiter, talent, HR, customer success, finance, legal, RevOps, partnerships, communications, brand, events, community, social media, design, CMO, GTM

### Included Title Keywords (Phase 2 MQL)
- engineering, engineer, platform, infrastructure, devex, developer experience
- AI, ML, machine learning, artificial intelligence, LLM
- data scientist, research engineer, applied scientist
- product, PM, developer, systems, backend, architect
- model, reliability, observability, scale, scalability, SRE

## 3. Qualification Criteria

### Jack Gardner's Qualification Framework (from Notion "Qualification" page)

**Volume / Potential Spend (Token-based)**
| Tier | Total Tokens/Month | Reaction |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| Low | < 1M | Go to Free/Pro, not enterprise |
| Med-low | < 1B | Pro tier unless they need support |
| Med | < 10B | Worth deeper conversation |
| Med-high | < 100B | Likely enterprise fit |
| High | > 100B | Definite enterprise fit |
| Mega high | > 1T | Top priority |

*Complexity coefficient:* 1 = simple chat, 3 = moderate agent, 5 = advanced agent

**AI Product Maturity**
| Stage | Reaction |
|-------|----------|
| Hackathon / Prototyping | Free/Pro only |
| Alpha | Pro unless support needed |
| Closed Beta & Beta | Talk more about usage & timelines |
| GA (Generally Available) | Enterprise fit |

**AI Use Case (External vs Internal)**
- External-facing, high-volume = best fit for enterprise
- Internal-only, low-volume = Free/Pro

**AI Hype / Brand Value**
- High funding + good investors + on billboards = stay close, get Slack channel started
- Low funding + never heard of them = Free/Pro

### Discovery Questions to Ask
1. What are your main AI applications/agents? Where are you in development? (alpha, beta, GA)
2. What's your current evals & observability setup?
3. What's your current volume? (tokens, traces, GB of data)
4. Are you okay with SaaS or is hybrid deployment needed?
5. What's your tech stack? (Python/TypeScript, frameworks, cloud provider)

### Additional Discovery (from persona pages)
- "How do you know changes won't break production?"
- "Can you trace why a specific response happened?"
- "How long to test a new model today?"
- "How do you ensure AI compliance across teams?"
- "What's your process when AI fails in production?"
- "How do PMs and engineers collaborate on AI today?"
- "How do you measure if AI changes actually improved things?"

## 6. Pain Points to Lead With on Calls

### By Persona

#### For Engineers (Staff+ AI/ML Engineers, VPs of Engineering)
1. **"How do you know a prompt change won't break production?"** -- Regression risk is the #1 pain. Lead with CI/CD quality gates that block bad deployments.
2. **"Can you trace why a specific response happened?"** -- Debugging agent workflows with 100s of steps is painful without proper observability.
3. **"How long does it take to test a new model?"** -- Braintrust offers instant availability of new models (test Claude/GPT day one). Competitors lag.
4. **"How many engineers maintain your homegrown eval system?"** -- Many teams have built DIY rigs that are brittle and expensive to maintain.
5. **"What happens when someone leaves and takes tribal eval knowledge with them?"** -- Standardized platform vs. custom scripts.

#### For Enterprise Platform Leaders (Directors+)
1. **"How do you ensure AI compliance across all teams?"** -- SOC2, RBAC, SSO, full audit trails, hybrid deployment.
2. **"How many teams are duplicating eval infrastructure work?"** -- One platform for all AI initiatives vs. fragmented tooling.
3. **"What's your process when AI fails in production?"** -- Non-blocking observability, regression detection, rollbacks.
4. **"Can your data stay in your VPC?"** -- Hybrid deployment model with customer VPC data plane. This is critical for regulated industries.
5. **"What's the cost of one production AI failure?"** -- Frame Braintrust as risk reduction, not just tooling.

#### For Product Managers
1. **"How do you measure if AI changes actually improved things?"** -- Replace "vibes-based" evaluation with measurable metrics.
2. **"Can you run experiments without waiting for engineering?"** -- No-code Playgrounds and Loop AI assistant.
3. **"How much time does your team spend on spreadsheets and manual reviews?"** -- Human review UI with keyboard shortcuts, annotation queues.
4. **"Can you see what engineers are testing?"** -- Shared platform with visibility across eng and product.
5. **"How do you make the business case for AI improvements to leadership?"** -- Executive dashboards, clear ROI metrics.

### Universal Pain Points (Any Persona)
- **Vendor lock-in**: Framework-agnostic, works with any model provider. No LangChain dependency.
- **Scale**: Brainstore handles terabytes with sub-second queries (24x faster than competitors on trace search).
- **Production observability without performance impact**: Non-blocking design -- production never stops.
- **Speed of iteration**: Test the latest Claude model the day it comes out. Competitors take days/weeks.

### 3 Key Messages to Memorize (from ICP doc)
1. **Engineers:** Catch regressions before users do with CI/CD gates
2. **Enterprises:** AI SDLC discipline -- treat AI like software with compliance
3. **PMs:** Make instinct-driven bets measurable without code

### Competitive Trap Questions (questions competitors cannot answer well)
- "Can you test the latest Claude model the day it comes out?" (Instant model availability)
- "Can you debug a trace with 1000 agent steps quickly?" (Brainstore speed)
- "Can you test local agents without deploying?" (Remote evals -- unique to Braintrust)
- "Can you run evals with zero data leaving your VPC?" (Hybrid deployment)
- "What happens if your observability layer goes down -- does production stop?" (Non-blocking design)
- "Can non-engineers run experiments?" (Playground)

### Objection Handling Quick Reference
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We built our own" | "How many engineers maintain it? What happens when they leave?" |
| "Using LangChain/LlamaIndex" | "2-line integration, keeps your existing code. No lock-in." |
| "Don't need this yet" | "What's your plan when the next model update breaks production?" |
| "Too expensive" | "What's the cost of one production AI failure? One compliance violation?" |
| "Need more security review" | "Here's our trust center, SOC2, and Fortune 500 references." |
| "Engineering owns this" | "PMs and engineers work in the same platform with shared visibility." |
| "Not technical enough" | "Loop AI does the technical work; you focus on quality." |
