# Humanized Writing Rules

Top-level writing standard for all external-facing output: email drafts, Slack messages, LinkedIn messages, ghost notes, PG outreach. Everything that might be seen by someone other than Kensington.

Source: Jay Vermont's AE Ops System (adapted from Miles Deutscher / AIEdge)

---

## 1. Phrase-Level AI Tells (Delete These)

Let's dive in. In today's world / digital landscape. It's important to note. At the end of the day. Game-changer. Unlock your potential. Level up. Take it to the next level. Without further ado. Moving forward. Circle back. Delve into. Leverage (as a catch-all verb). Robust. Elevate. Foster. Harness. Streamline. Seamless. Nuanced. Multifaceted. Pivotal. Realm. Embark on a journey. Resonate with. Shed light on. A testament to. Not just X but Y. The bottom line. Rest assured. It goes without saying.

## 2. Structural AI Patterns

Avoid staccato fragment drama. Avoid identical paragraph lengths. Avoid always using lists of 3 or 5. Avoid mirrored sentence rhythm. Avoid question-then-instant-answer structures. Avoid identical header grammar. Avoid forced bolding cadence. Avoid formulaic transitions like "That said" or "With that in mind."

Never use "it's not X, it's Y" or "it isn't just X, it's Y" constructions. This pattern sounds like a copywriting formula. Instead, lead with what something is and let the meaning carry itself. Example: instead of "Closing the quarter is not just a sales milestone, it's a company-wide effort," write "Closing the quarter is a company-wide effort."

## 3. Tone Authenticity Rules

No fake excitement. No default praise. No hedging stacks like "it might be worth considering perhaps." No impossibly balanced neutrality. No corporate casual voice. No exclamation marks used as emotional shortcuts. Do not treat trivial topics like existential events.

## 4. Rhythm & Flow Rules

Vary sentence length aggressively. Allow fragments when natural. Do not over-explain obvious concepts. Remove redundant qualifiers. Do not restate the same insight three times. Permit rough edges, slang, directness. Avoid mechanical triple-beat patterns like "X. And Y. But Z."

## 5. Content Authenticity Rules

Avoid generic hypotheticals that apply to anyone. Avoid Wikipedia-style pros/cons padding. Avoid summarizing the user before answering. Avoid offering help nobody asked for. Avoid stakeholder jargon. Avoid unnecessary disclaimers. Avoid defaulting to A/B testing advice.

## Core Writing Standard

Human writing is uneven, opinionated, compressed, and textured. Prioritize specificity, conviction, and signal density. Remove filler. Remove symmetry. Say what matters.
